THE MISSILE MAN

The term "Missile Man of India" refers to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, who served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007. Dr. Kalam was an aerospace engineer and played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts. He earned the nickname "Missile Man" for his significant contributions to the development of ballistic missile technology in India.

INDIAN SPACE RESEARCH ORGANISATION

Dr. Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, and he passed away on July 27, 2015. Before serving as the President, he had a distinguished career as a scientist and engineer, working at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). He played a key role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle and the operationalization of strategic missiles. Dr. Kalam was not only a brilliant scientist but also a popular and inspirational figure. He was known for his vision of India's development and his efforts to promote science and technology among the youth. His contributions to India's space and defense programs, as well as his advocacy for education, earned him widespread respect and admiration.
